Porcelain vs Ceramic Tiles: Key Differences for Importers

Learn the main differences between porcelain and ceramic tiles for importers, distributors and construction projects.

Porcelain and ceramic tiles are both widely used in residential and commercial projects, but they are not exactly the same. Understanding the difference helps importers and distributors choose the right products for each market.

What Are Porcelain Tiles?

Porcelain tiles are generally denser, stronger and less absorbent than standard ceramic tiles. They are commonly used for floors, commercial areas, high-traffic spaces and modern architectural projects.

What Are Ceramic Tiles?

Ceramic tiles are practical, versatile and often more economical. They are widely used for walls, bathrooms, kitchens, residential floors and renovation projects.

Which One Is Better?

The best choice depends on the project. Porcelain tiles are usually preferred for durability and premium finishes. Ceramic tiles are often selected when buyers need cost-effective wall or floor solutions.

Popular Sizes

Porcelain tiles are common in 60x60, 60x120, 80x80 and 100x100 formats. Ceramic tiles are popular in 30x30, 30x60, 30x90, 40x40 and 50x50 sizes.

Choosing Tiles for Your Market

Importers should consider target price, design preference, installation habits, building standards and customer demand in their country. LuxeCeram can help buyers compare porcelain and ceramic tile options based on their market needs.
