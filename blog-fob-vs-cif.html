FOB vs CIF for Tile Imports: Simple Guide for Buyers

Simple guide to FOB and CIF shipping terms for porcelain and ceramic tile importers.

When importing porcelain and ceramic tiles, buyers often receive quotations based on FOB or CIF terms. Understanding these terms helps avoid confusion and makes price comparison easier.

What Does FOB Mean?

FOB means Free On Board. In simple terms, the seller is responsible for delivering the goods to the departure port and loading them on the vessel. After that point, the buyer usually manages sea freight, insurance and destination costs.

What Does CIF Mean?

CIF means Cost, Insurance and Freight. In this case, the seller includes the cost of goods, insurance and sea freight to the destination port in the quotation.

Which Option Is Better?

FOB is useful for buyers who already have a trusted freight forwarder. CIF can be easier for buyers who want a more complete price to the destination port.

Tile Import Cost Factors

Tile import costs may include product price, packaging, palletizing, inland transport, port charges, sea freight, insurance, customs clearance and local delivery at destination.
